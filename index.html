AUDI R8

The Audi R8 is a mid-engine, 2-seater sports car, which uses Audi's trademark quattro permanent all-wheel drive system. ... The car is built by Audi Sport GmbH in a newly renovated factory at Audi's 'aluminium site' at Neckarsulm in Germany. It is also the first production car with full-LED headlamps

RANGE ROVER VELAR

Range Rover Velar leads the way in reductive design. Clean, elegant and distinctive, the vehicle has a striking, contemporary presence. Refined power and effortless elegance combine with exquisite effect in this outstanding example of contemporary automotive design.

BMW I8 M

The i8 M's plug-in hybrid powertrain is anticipated to follow the same basic setup as the current i8, which means an electric motor driving the front wheels and a mid-mounted gasoline engine and electric motor for the rear wheels. In lieu of the i8's turbocharged 1.5-liter three-cylinder gas engine, BMW has said the new car will offer a turbocharged four-cylinder

LAMBORGHINI AVENTADOR

The Aventador LP700–4 uses Lamborghini's new 700 PS (515 kW; 690 bhp) 6,498 cc (6.5 L; 396.5 cu in) 60° V12 engine weighing 235 kg. Known internally as the L539, the new engine is Lamborghini's fifth in-house engine and second V12 design. It is the first all-new V12 since the 3.5 litre powerplant found in the 350GT.

PORSCHE 911 TURBO

The 911 Turbo's 3.8-liter twin-turbo flat-six engine produces 572 hp and 553 lb-ft of torque and combines with all-wheel drive and an eight-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission to blitz to 60 mph in just 2.7 seconds with the Sport Chrono Package. ... Inside, it gains 18-way adaptive sport seats.
